# Knowledge-synthesis with the interdisciplinary approach of Hierarchology and hierarchography
Hierarchology is the interdisciplinary science that investigates hierarchical associative systems across human cognition, social structures, and natural ecosystems, whereas hierarchography (coined by Karl Petri훾 2022) denotes the methodologies for describing, visualizing, and archiving these hierarchies through multifaceted data representations. Hierarchography encompasses the methods and techniques for describing, visualizing, and recording hierarchical associative systems, as a complement to hierarchology.
These techniques employ hybrid approaches, integrating verbal, pictorial or visual, auditory, tactile, and motor data forms to capture and represent intricate relationships within such systems.[5]
Elaborated in Petri훾's 2025 publication, hierarchography facilitates model construction and precise delineation of interconnections, distinguishing itself from hierarchology's emphasis on systemic analysis by focusing on representational and documentation practices.
